Phonics for You

Level: Basic | 30 hours (1.5 hrs/class) | Click any card for details

Unit 1: The Alphabet

Letter recognition and basic sounds.

1

Alphabet Slap

Race to swat the correct letter on the board when the sound is called.

Flyswatters, letter flashcards.
  1. Stick A-Z flashcards on the board.
  2. Two teams line up.
  3. Teacher says a letter sound (e.g., /b/).
  4. Students race to swat 'B'.
Say the *sound* not the *name* of the letter to reinforce phonics.
2

Letter Match

Match uppercase letters to lowercase letters.

Uppercase and lowercase letter cards.
  1. Scatter uppercase cards on one side of the room, lowercase on the other.
  2. Give a student an uppercase card.
  3. They must run and find the matching lowercase card.
Have them shout the letter sound once they find the match.
3

Alphabet Soup

Draw a letter from a bowl and say a word that starts with it.

A bowl, plastic letters or small letter cards.
  1. Pass the "soup bowl" around while playing music.
  2. Stop the music. The student holding the bowl pulls out a letter.
  3. They must say the sound and a word (e.g., "/c/ Cat!").
For beginners, put pictures on the board they can use as hints.
4

Air Writing

Write letters in the air for classmates to guess.

None.
  1. Teacher silently writes a large letter in the air with their finger.
  2. Students guess the letter and shout its sound.
  3. Let students take turns coming to the front to be the "writer".
Make sure they write facing the class so it doesn't look backward.

Unit 2: Consonants Pairs

Practicing paired consonant sounds.

1

Sound Sorting

Sort picture cards by their beginning consonant sound.

Picture cards, two baskets labeled with letters (e.g., B and P).
  1. Show a picture (e.g., Pig).
  2. Ask students: "Does this start with /p/ or /b/?"
  3. Students race to put the card in the correct basket.
Focus on voiced vs. unvoiced pairs (like B/P, D/T) to help them feel the throat vibration.
2

Consonant Charades

Act out an animal or object starting with a specific consonant.

Consonant flashcards.
  1. Hold up a letter (e.g., 'M').
  2. Students must act like something starting with M (Monkey, Monster).
  3. Teacher guesses what they are acting out.
This gets them moving. Model an example first so they know what to do.
3

Mystery Bag

Pull a real object from a bag and identify its first sound.

A bag, real items (pen, book, cup).
  1. Have a student close their eyes and reach into the bag.
  2. They pull out an item and name it.
  3. They must shout the beginning consonant sound.
Realia (real objects) makes phonics highly engaging for beginners.
4

Four Corners

Run to the corner that matches the starting sound.

4 letter cards placed in room corners.
  1. Label the 4 corners of the room with different consonants.
  2. Call out a word.
  3. Students must run to the corner with the matching starting letter.
Keep the words simple and emphasize the first sound heavily.

Unit 3: Other Consonants

Remaining consonants and sound mastery.

1

Sound Hopscotch

Jump on the correct letter when you hear the sound.

Large letter cards taped to the floor.
  1. Tape letters on the floor like a hopscotch grid.
  2. Say a sound.
  3. Student must jump directly onto that letter.
Use tape that doesn't ruin the floor! Great for kinesthetic learners.
2

Pass the Sound

Pass a card and change the sound quickly.

Letter cards.
  1. Sit in a circle. Hand a letter card to Student A.
  2. Student A says the sound and passes it.
  3. When teacher claps, change the letter card.
Speeds up response time and automaticity.
3

Flyswatter Review

Hit the picture that matches the consonant.

Flyswatters, picture cards.
  1. Put pictures of objects on the board.
  2. Call out a consonant sound (e.g., /v/).
  3. Teams race to swat the 'Van'.
Using pictures instead of letters trains them to map sounds to words.
4

Write the Room

Walk around and find hidden letters to write down.

Clipboards, paper, hidden letter cards.
  1. Hide consonant cards around the room.
  2. Give students a paper.
  3. They must walk around, find the letters, and write them down.
After they find them all, have them read the sounds back to you.

Unit 4: Short Vowels

Focusing on short a, e, i, o, u.

1

Vowel Jump

Jump when you hear the target short vowel sound.

None.
  1. Tell the class: "Jump when you hear the short A sound (/æ/)."
  2. Read a list of words slowly: bed, pig, CAT!
  3. Students must jump on 'Cat'.
Great for ear training. Exaggerate the middle vowel sound.
2

CVC Word Maker

Use letter cards to build simple 3-letter words.

Consonant and Vowel letter cards.
  1. Give groups a set of letters.
  2. Teacher says a word: "Sun".
  3. Groups race to arrange the cards: S - U - N.
Use blue cards for consonants and red cards for vowels to make the CVC pattern obvious.
3

Vowel Pop

Tap the correct vowel balloon on the smartboard.

Whiteboard with 5 vowels drawn inside balloons.
  1. Draw 5 balloons on the board (a, e, i, o, u).
  2. Say a CVC word.
  3. Student runs up and "pops" (taps) the correct middle vowel.
Short E and Short I are very hard to distinguish. Give extra practice with those two.
4

Middle Sound Sort

Sort picture cards by their middle vowel sound.

Picture cards of CVC words, 5 baskets.
  1. Label 5 baskets with a, e, i, o, u.
  2. Give pairs a stack of picture cards (dog, cat, bed).
  3. They must sort the pictures into the correct vowel basket.
Check answers together. Have them stretch the word out like a rubber band: ddd-ooo-ggg.

Unit 5: Silent E Rule

Magic E makes the vowel say its name.

1

Magic Wand E

Use a magic wand to change a short vowel word to a long vowel word.

A star wand with an 'e' taped to it, word cards.
  1. Write 'tap' on the board.
  2. Student reads "tap".
  3. Tap the end of the word with the Magic 'e' wand.
  4. Student must read the new word: "tape".
The visual aid of the wand helps concrete the concept of the 'magic' change.
2

Silent E Flip

Foldable cards that hide and reveal the silent e.

Foldable index cards.
  1. Write CVC words on cards, with an extra folded flap for 'e'.
  2. Show 'plan'. Student reads it.
  3. Flip the flap open to reveal 'plane'. Student reads again.
Great for independent station work.
3

CVCe Bingo

Bingo game using only Silent E words.

Bingo cards with CVCe words.
  1. Give students bingo grids filled with words like cake, bike, home.
  2. Call out the words clearly.
  3. First to get a line wins.
Call out "kit" as a trick. If they cross out "kite", correct them!
4

Word Transformation

Relay race to add 'e' to words on the board.

Whiteboard, markers.
  1. Write a list of CVC words on the board for two teams (cub, mad, pin).
  2. Students race up and add an 'e' to the end.
  3. They must read the new word aloud to score.
Make sure the base CVC words actually form real CVCe words!

Unit 6: Long Vowels Ⅰ

Vowel teams like ai, ay, ee, ea.

1

Vowel Team Sort

Sort word cards into their correct vowel team columns.

Word cards with ai, ay, ee, ea.
  1. Draw columns on the board for 'ai' and 'ay'.
  2. Give students word cards (rain, play, day, train).
  3. They must stick them in the right column and read them.
Teach the rule: 'ay' usually comes at the end of a word, 'ai' in the middle.
2

Team Relay

Run to the board to fill in the missing vowel team.

Whiteboard.
  1. Write incomplete words: s _ _ d (seed), p l _ _ (play).
  2. Teams run up and fill in the missing 2 vowels.
  3. Read the word aloud to win.
Remind them "When two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking."
3

Picture Dictation

Teacher dictates a word, students spell it and draw it.

Mini whiteboards for students.
  1. Say: "Tree".
  2. Students must write T-R-E-E and draw a quick tree.
  3. Hold up boards to check spelling.
Drawing connects the abstract spelling to concrete meaning.
4

Long Vowel Slap

Listen to the word and swat the correct vowel team.

Flyswatters, flashcards of vowel teams.
  1. Put cards for EE, EA, AI, AY on the board.
  2. Say a word: "Meat!"
  3. Students swat the 'EA' card.
Since EE and EA sound the same, you may need to show them the picture to help them guess the spelling.

Unit 7: Long Vowels Ⅱ

Vowel teams like ie, igh, oa, ow.

1

Vowel Board Game

Roll dice and read words with Long O and Long I.

Printed board game, dice, tokens.
  1. Groups of 3-4 play a board game.
  2. Land on a square (e.g., 'boat').
  3. Must read the word correctly to stay on the square.
Include 'igh' words (night, high) as these are usually visually confusing for beginners.
2

Whisper Down the Alley

Whisper a long vowel word down a line to test phonics clarity.

None.
  1. Line up students.
  2. Whisper a word like "Snow" to the first student.
  3. They whisper it to the next.
  4. The last student must write it on the board.
Ensure they enunciate the long vowel clearly when whispering.
3

Sound Match Memory

Play memory match with pictures and Long Vowel II words.

Word and picture cards.
  1. Cards face down.
  2. Flip two cards. If 'pie' matches the picture of a pie, keep it.
  3. Student must read the word out loud.
Excellent for quiet, focused center work.
4

Vowel Search

Find hidden long vowel words in a letter grid.

Printed word search worksheets.
  1. Give students a word search.
  2. They must find words like coat, tie, light, window.
  3. Circle them and read them to a partner.
Keeps students looking at letter combinations (igh, oa) repeatedly, building visual memory.

Unit 8: Consonant Digraphs

Two consonants, one sound: sh, ch, th, wh.

1

Digraph Sort

Sort picture cards under the correct digraph heading.

Headers (sh, ch, th, wh) and picture cards.
  1. Place headers on the board.
  2. Give pairs picture cards (ship, chair, thumb, whale).
  3. Sort them into the correct columns.
Make sure to practice voiced vs unvoiced 'th' (this vs. thumb).
2

Digraph Slap

Listen to the teacher and swat the correct digraph.

Flyswatters, large digraph cards.
  1. Put SH, CH, TH, WH on the board.
  2. Say a word: "Cheese!".
  3. Students swat 'CH'.
Sh and Ch are often confused by ESL learners. Exaggerate the explosive sound of 'CH'.
3

Digraph Hunt

Search the classroom for objects containing digraphs.

Post-it notes.
  1. Tell students to find items with SH, CH, TH.
  2. They put post-its on them (Shoe, Chair, Trash).
  3. Review all found items together.
Pre-hide some picture cards around the room if there aren't enough real objects.
4

Mystery Digraph

Fill in the missing digraph to complete the word.

Mini whiteboards.
  1. Write incomplete words on the board: __ ip.
  2. Hold up a picture of a Ship.
  3. Students write 'SH' on their whiteboards and hold them up.
Make it a race for extra excitement.

Unit 9: Hard And Soft C,G

C says /s/, G says /j/ before e, i, y.

1

Hard/Soft Sort

Categorize words based on whether they have a hard or soft sound.

Word cards (cat, city, gate, gym).
  1. Draw a 'Hard C' and 'Soft C' column.
  2. Students read a word card.
  3. They tape it in the correct column based on the sound.
Teach them to highlight the letter immediately following C or G to decide the sound.
2

Sound Hop

Jump to the left for hard sounds, right for soft sounds.

None.
  1. Draw a line down the middle of the room.
  2. Say a word: "Giraffe!"
  3. Students hop to the 'Soft G' side. Say "Goat!" -> hop to the 'Hard G' side.
Great TPR (Total Physical Response) activity to break up sitting time.
3

Word Reading Race

Relay race to read hard and soft words correctly.

List of words on the board.
  1. Write mixed C and G words on the board.
  2. Students run up one by one.
  3. They must point and read a word correctly to score.
If they mispronounce 'magic' with a hard G, make them run back and try again next turn.
4

Circle the Clue

Find the vowels that make C and G soft.

Worksheets.
  1. Give students a list of words.
  2. They must circle the 'e', 'i', or 'y' that follows the C or G.
  3. Then read the word to a partner.
Visualizing the rule is vital. Circling forces them to look at the rule in action.

Unit 10: Consonant Blends

Blends where both sounds are heard: bl, cr, st, etc.

1

Blend Match

Match the consonant blend to the rest of the word.

Blend cards (bl, tr) and ending cards (ack, ee).
  1. Give students a stack of blend beginnings.
  2. They must match them to word endings to form real words (bl + ack = black).
  3. Read the new word.
Explain that unlike digraphs, you hear BOTH letters in a blend quickly.
2

Board Race

Race to write the missing blend on the board.

Whiteboard, markers.
  1. Write words missing their blends: __ ock (block/clock).
  2. Say the word aloud.
  3. Students race to write the correct blend in the blank.
Accept multiple correct answers if they form real words, as long as they read it correctly!
3

Mystery Blend

Listen to a word and hold up the correct blend card.

Mini blend cards for each student.
  1. Give each student 'sl', 'fl', 'cl' cards.
  2. Say: "Flag".
  3. Students must quickly hold up the 'fl' card.
This lets you check the entire class's understanding simultaneously.
4

Blend Bingo

Classic Bingo using only words that start with consonant blends.

Bingo boards.
  1. Students fill bingo boards with blend words (frog, stop, tree).
  2. Call out words.
  3. First to get a line shouts Bingo.
Make them spell the winning words back to you to verify.

Unit 11: Silent Letters

Letters you see but don't hear: kn, wr, mb.

1

Ghost Letter Hunt

Find words with silent letters and circle the "ghost" letter.

Worksheets.
  1. Give a text filled with silent letter words (knee, write, comb).
  2. Students read and draw a little 'ghost' over the silent letter.
  3. Read the words aloud.
Calling them "Ghost Letters" helps younger learners remember they are invisible to the ear.
2

Silent Letter Slap

Swat the correct spelling when the teacher says a word.

Flyswatters.
  1. Write pairs on the board: "nite/knight", "rite/write".
  2. Say the word.
  3. Teams must swat the correct, silent-letter spelling.
This trains visual memory, which is essential for silent letters.
3

Spelling Relay

Run to the board to spell words with silent letters correctly.

Whiteboard, markers.
  1. Say a word: "Thumb".
  2. Students run and write it.
  3. If they forget the 'b', their team helps them fix it.
Review the common combinations (kn, wr, mb, gn) before starting.
4

Silent Word Match

Match the picture to the silent letter word.

Cards.
  1. Place pictures on the table.
  2. Students match words like 'knife', 'wreath', 'lamb' to the pictures.
  3. Read them aloud.
Focus on vocabulary building here as some of these words are less common.

Unit 12: Vowel Digraph

More complex vowel teams producing one sound.

1

Digraph Memory

Play memory matching words with the same vowel digraph.

Word cards.
  1. Turn cards face down.
  2. Flip two. If they share a digraph (e.g., Moon, Spoon), keep them.
  3. Must read both words aloud.
Great for focusing on the middle sounds of words.
2

Digraph Sort

Sort words into different vowel digraph columns.

Word cards, board.
  1. Draw columns for 'oo' (book), 'oo' (moon), 'ew', etc.
  2. Students tape words in the correct column.
Highlight that 'oo' makes two different sounds (look vs. boot).
3

Sound Bingo

Bingo focused on vowel digraphs.

Bingo boards.
  1. Students fill board with digraph words.
  2. Teacher says the word.
  3. Cross it out until Bingo.
Encourage students to spell the word out loud when they get bingo to confirm.
4

Digraph Dictation

Listen to the teacher and write the correct vowel digraph word.

Mini whiteboards.
  1. Say a word: "Foot".
  2. Students write the whole word.
  3. Hold up boards. Correct any spelling mistakes immediately.
This checks if they can distinguish between similar sounds and spell them accurately.

Unit 13: Diphthongs Ⅰ

Gliding vowels: oi, oy, ou, ow.

1

Diphthong Hop

Jump on the correct diphthong card.

Floor cards (oi, oy, ou, ow).
  1. Place cards on the floor.
  2. Say a word: "Cow!".
  3. Student hops onto 'ow'.
Teach the rule: 'oy' usually ends a word, 'oi' is in the middle. Same for 'ow' and 'ou'.
2

Word Maker

Combine consonants with diphthongs to build words.

Letter cards.
  1. Give pairs consonant cards and diphthong cards.
  2. Teacher says: "Coin".
  3. Students build C-OI-N.
This visual building helps cement spelling patterns.
3

Picture Match

Match diphthong words to their pictures.

Word and picture cards.
  1. Scatter words and pictures.
  2. Students find the match (e.g., 'Boy' text + Boy picture).
  3. Read word aloud.
Good for individual practice or quiet time.
4

Flyswatter

Swat the correct diphthong word.

Flyswatters.
  1. Write words on board: boil, toy, house, town.
  2. Say the sound: /oy/.
  3. Students swat 'toy' or 'boil'.
Make them say the word after they hit it to practice the gliding sound.

Unit 14: Diphthongs Ⅱ

More complex vowel sounds like au, aw.

1

Sound Sort

Sort words into 'au' and 'aw' categories.

Word cards, columns on board.
  1. Draw 'au' and 'aw' columns.
  2. Students take a card (e.g., saw, August).
  3. Place it in correct column and read.
Explain 'aw' usually comes at the end of syllables/words, while 'au' is in the middle.
2

Diphthong Slap

Swat the correct spelling pattern when heard.

Flyswatters.
  1. Put 'au' and 'aw' on board.
  2. Say "Claw!".
  3. Students swat 'aw'.
These sound identical, so focus on visual spelling rules.
3

Word Reading Race

Relay race to read diphthong words correctly.

Board.
  1. Write a list of au/aw words.
  2. Teams race up.
  3. Read the word aloud correctly to score.
Ensure they drop their jaw to make the correct /ɔ/ sound.
4

Diphthong Bingo

Play bingo with au/aw vocabulary.

Bingo cards.
  1. Fill board with target words.
  2. Call out words.
  3. First to get a line wins.
Ask the winner to write one of their winning words on the board.

Unit 15: R Control Vowels Ⅰ

Bossy R sounds: ar, er.

1

Bossy R Sort

Sort words into 'ar' and 'er' columns.

Word cards.
  1. Draw 'ar' (car) and 'er' (tiger) on board.
  2. Give out cards.
  3. Students tape them in correct columns.
Remind them 'R' is bossy and changes the vowel sound completely.
2

Bossy R Slap

Listen to the word and swat 'ar' or 'er'.

Flyswatters.
  1. Put AR and ER on the board.
  2. Say "Star!".
  3. Students swat AR.
Make them repeat the word with a big "pirate" ARRR sound for fun.
3

Word Search

Find hidden 'ar' and 'er' words.

Word search worksheets.
  1. Hand out worksheets.
  2. Find target words (park, farm, sister, water).
  3. Circle and read them.
Focuses visual attention on the spelling pattern.
4

Mystery Bossy R

Fill in the missing R-controlled vowel.

Whiteboards.
  1. Write: f __ m.
  2. Show picture of a Farm.
  3. Students write 'ar' on whiteboards.
Check all whiteboards to ensure everyone is participating.

Unit 16: R Control Vowels Ⅱ

Bossy R sounds: ir, or, ur.

1

Bossy R Hop

Jump to the correct ir, or, ur card.

Floor cards.
  1. Place ir, or, ur on the floor.
  2. Say "Fork!".
  3. Student jumps on 'or'.
Explain that 'ir' and 'ur' sound exactly the same, so they must memorize the spelling!
2

Bossy R Bingo

Bingo game for ir, or, ur words.

Bingo cards.
  1. Fill board with words (bird, corn, nurse).
  2. Call out words.
  3. First line wins.
Spell the word when calling it out to help them distinguish 'ir' and 'ur'.
3

Picture Dictation

Spell the word and draw the picture.

Whiteboards.
  1. Say "Shirt".
  2. Students write S-H-I-R-T and draw it.
  3. Check whiteboards.
Great for combining digraphs (sh) with R-controlled vowels (ir).
4

Sound Sort

Sort words into ir, or, ur columns.

Word cards.
  1. Draw columns.
  2. Students tape words in correct column.
  3. Read aloud.
Highlight the target letters with a different colored marker.

Unit 17: R Control Vowels Ⅲ

Review of all Bossy R sounds.

1

Bossy R Review

A comprehensive board game covering ar, er, ir, or, ur.

Board game, dice.
  1. Groups play a board game.
  2. Must read the R-controlled word correctly to stay on the square.
Encourage peer correction. If they read 'car' as 'care', the group can correct them.
2

Flyswatter Mix

Swat the correct word among all Bossy R types.

Flyswatters.
  1. Write mixed words (car, term, bird, fork, surf) on board.
  2. Say a word.
  3. Teams race to swat it.
This tests visual discrimination since many sound similar.
3

Spelling Relay

Run and write the spelling dictated by the teacher.

Board.
  1. Say "Nurse!".
  2. Student runs and writes 'nurse'.
  3. Team gets point if correct.
er, ir, and ur sound the same. This is a pure spelling memory test.
4

Word Match

Match all types of R-controlled words to pictures.

Cards.
  1. Scatter pictures and words.
  2. Students match them up.
  3. Read aloud to the teacher to finish.
A quiet synthesis activity to end the Bossy R series.

Unit 18: Y as a Vowel

Y sounding like long 'i' (fly) or long 'e' (happy).

1

Y Sound Sort

Sort words by Y = /i/ or Y = /e/.

Word cards.
  1. Draw columns for 'Y sounds like I' and 'Y sounds like E'.
  2. Students read cards (cry, puppy).
  3. Tape in correct column.
Teach the rule: Short words (cry, my) usually make the 'I' sound. Longer words (happy, candy) make the 'E' sound.
2

Y Slap

Listen and swat the 'I' or 'E' card.

Flyswatters.
  1. Put 'I' and 'E' on the board.
  2. Say "Baby".
  3. Students swat 'E'.
Helps them train their ears to the vowel sound Y makes at the end of words.
3

Word Reading Relay

Race to read Y words on the board.

Board.
  1. Write list of Y words.
  2. Run up, point, and read perfectly.
  3. Run back.
Make sure they read 'sky' with an 'I' sound and 'funny' with an 'E' sound.
4

Y Bingo

Bingo with 'Y as a vowel' vocabulary.

Bingo boards.
  1. Fill board with words like sky, fly, happy, silly.
  2. Call out words.
  3. First to get a line wins.
Call out "Puppy". Have students repeat the word after crossing it out.

Unit 19 & 20: Syllables Ⅰ& II

Breaking words into sound chunks.

1

Syllable Clap

Listen to a word and clap out the number of syllables.

None.
  1. Say a word: "Apple".
  2. Model clapping: Ap-ple (2 claps).
  3. Say "Elephant" -> El-e-phant (3 claps).
Teach them the "chin drop" method. Put hand under chin; how many times does the jaw drop?
2

Syllable Sort

Sort word cards into 1, 2, or 3 syllable groups.

Word cards.
  1. Draw columns: 1, 2, 3.
  2. Students read card (e.g., banana).
  3. Clap it out, tape it in column '3'.
Visually underline the vowels on the cards to show that every syllable needs a vowel sound.
3

Syllable Hop

Hop forward for every syllable in a word.

None.
  1. Line students up.
  2. Say a word: "Computer".
  3. Students must hop 3 times.
If they hop the wrong amount, they have to go back to the start!
4

Word Building

Put broken syllables together to make a full word.

Cards with single syllables (tig + er).
  1. Give pairs broken words (pa + per, doc + tor).
  2. They must match them to form real words.
  3. Read them aloud.
This trains them to read long words by breaking them into manageable chunks.

Unit 21 & 22: Linking Sound & Intonation

Reading sentences smoothly with emotion.

1

Linking Sound Read

Practice linking a consonant ending to a vowel beginning.

Sentences on board.
  1. Write: "It is an apple."
  2. Draw a link from 't' to 'i', 's' to 'a', 'n' to 'a'.
  3. Have them read it smoothly: "I-ti-za-napple."
This helps eliminate robotic, word-by-word reading.
2

Intonation Echo

Listen to the teacher's pitch and repeat exactly.

None.
  1. Say a question with rising pitch: "Are you okay? ↗"
  2. Students must echo the exact pitch.
  3. Say a statement: "I am fine. ↘"
Use hand gestures (moving hand up or down) to visually show the pitch changing.
3

Sentence Relay

Read a full sentence with linking and intonation to score.

Sentences on board.
  1. Write sentences with ? and ! on the board.
  2. Students race up.
  3. Must read the sentence smoothly with correct emotion to win a point.
If they read it like a robot, send them back to try again!
4

Emotion Reading

Read the same sentence using different emotional tones.

Emotion cards (Happy, Sad, Angry, Scared).
  1. Write: "Look at that dog."
  2. Show an 'Angry' card. Student reads it angrily.
  3. Show 'Happy' card. Read it happily.
This proves they understand that punctuation and tone change the entire meaning of English.
